# Impact of AI on healthcare 2024

## Executive Summary
The integration of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the healthcare sector is expected to revolutionize the industry in 2024. According to [1], the healthcare industry will focus more on healthcare rather than sick care, with AI being used to help keep patients healthy. The use of AI in healthcare is expected to grow considerably, with 79% of healthcare organizations already adopting AI technology in some capacity [4]. As AI continues to evolve, it is likely to have a profound impact on the healthcare industry, improving patient care, streamlining administrative tasks, and reducing costs.

## Key Finding 1 · Expansion of AI in Healthcare
The use of AI in healthcare is expected to expand into different medical specialties in 2024 [1]. AI will be used to help diagnose diseases, develop personalized treatment plans, and improve patient outcomes. For instance, AI modeling can help doctors spot signs of cancer or heart disease quicker than before, ensuring better prognosis for patients and potentially saving governments millions of dollars [2]. Additionally, AI can help summarize medical research and clinical notes, reducing the time it takes to develop healthcare technologies [3].

## Key Finding 2 · Streamlining Administrative Tasks
Another key benefit of AI in healthcare is its ability to streamline administrative tasks, reducing costs and allowing medical staff to focus more on patient care [2]. AI can perform mundane tasks that consume healthcare professionals' time, helping to address chronic staffing problems at hospitals [3]. According to [5], the three main benefits of AI in healthcare are improving the accuracy and speed of medical diagnoses, tailoring treatments to individual patients, and streamlining administrative tasks.

## Key Finding 3 · Future of AI in Healthcare
The future of AI in healthcare is expected to be significant, with AI becoming more deeply integrated into patient care, both in terms of storing patient data and treatment [2]. The global marketplace value of AI in healthcare is projected to grow from $27 billion in 2024 to over $613 billion by 2034 [12]. AI is expected to help bolster medical education and specialty training, with medical students being certified sooner and having the tools to be more productive in healthcare [12].

## Key Finding 4 · Current Applications of AI in Healthcare
AI is currently being used in various processes in healthcare, including analyzing complex medical data, improving diagnostic accuracy, creating personalized treatment plans, and enhancing operational efficiency [4]. According to [8], AI can help public health officials allocate resources, implement prevention strategies, and tackle emergent health threats. Additionally, AI can help improve patient outcomes, reduce costs, and enhance the overall quality of care [11].

## Challenges and Risks
Despite the benefits of AI in healthcare, there are several challenges and risks associated with its adoption. These include concerns around data quality, workforce acceptance, and compliance with regulatory requirements [13]. There are also concerns about the potential loss of human interaction, overreliance on AI for diagnosis, and security, ethics, and risk [13]. According to [14], stakeholders must actively engage in discussions to address these challenges and ensure that AI in healthcare aligns with ethical standards and prioritizes patient well-being.

## Conclusion
In conclusion, the impact of AI on healthcare in 2024 is expected to be significant, with AI being used to improve patient care, streamline administrative tasks, and reduce costs. While there are challenges and risks associated with the adoption of AI in healthcare, these can be addressed through collaborative effort and a thoughtful approach to implementation. As AI continues to evolve, it is likely to have a profound impact on the healthcare industry, improving patient outcomes and enhancing the overall quality of care.
